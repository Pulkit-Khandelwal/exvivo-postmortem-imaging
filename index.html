Biomedical image segmentation

What is this tutorial about?

Years ago, when I first was first introduced to the field of biomedical image analyis, I was scared by the number of papers out there. In particular, I was overwhelmed by how different research disciplines were organized and structured within biomedical imaging. I had no clue where to start, i.e., which key papers, books to read, and where to go next from there. Therefore, here, I present a nove overview of medical image segmentation using deep learning (I plan to make another set of videos soon for segmentation methods before the deep learning era). In this two part tutorial, we will follow a systematic approach starting from U-Net and its variants to build a solid foundation and eventually by the end of part 2 of the tutorial, we will be able to talk about advanced architectures such as Transformers. Thus, we will have a whirlwind tour of state of the art papers in deep medical image segmentation.

Tutorial 1 covers the following topics:

  • Building foundational building blocks via the key papers
  • Domain adaptation and generalization
  • Meta-learning
  • Attention mechanisms
  • Generative modeling and synthesis
  • Domain disentanglement

Tutorial 2 covers the following topics:

  • Model distillation (student-teacher networks)
  • Weakly-supervised and few-shot learning
  • Representation learning, self-supervised and multi-task learning
  • Shape-priors, topology-aware and geometrically-constrained networks
  • Transformers
